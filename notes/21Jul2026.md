## Technical Report: Turbulence Closure Mechanics in `scm/scm.jl`

### Executive Summary

The turbulence closure implemented in `scm/scm.jl` is a $C^\infty$-smooth, non-local, manifold-embedded model based on **Geometrical Singular Perturbation Theory (GSPT)**. Specifically designed for Stable Boundary Layers (SBL), it models abrupt collapses and recoveries of turbulence (fold catastrophes) while maintaining complete continuous differentiability ($C^\infty$) across phase space transitions. ## 1. GSPT Option 2 Fold Catastrophe Dynamics

At the core of the closure is a fast-slow dynamical system representing the competition between shear production, thermal stratifying destruction, and non-linear dissipation.

### Net Forcing ($\Delta$)

The local forcing balance $\Delta$ combines vertical wind shear squared ($S^2$) and thermal stability suppression ($G$):

$$S^2 = \left(\frac{\partial U}{\partial z}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial z}\right)^2$$

$$\Delta = \eta S^2 - K_{\text{buoy}} G$$

* $\eta$: Shear production efficiency.
* $K_{\text{buoy}}$: Buoyancy destruction coupling constant.
* $G$: Bounded stability response function.

### Manifold Discriminant ($D$) & Hyperbolic Embedding ($\xi$)

In standard fold catastrophes, the state manifold branch point occurs at $D = 0$. To prevent slope discontinuities ($\frac{\partial}{\partial D}\sqrt{D} \to \infty$), the implementation embeds the manifold into a regularized hyperbolic space parameterised by $\xi > 0$:

$$D = \beta_{\text{gspt}}^2 + 4 \Delta$$

$$\sqrt{D_{\text{reg}}} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{2}\left( D + \sqrt{D^2 + \xi^2} \right)}$$

$$H_{\text{step}} = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 + \frac{D}{\sqrt{D^2 + \xi^2}}\right)$$

$$q^* = H_{\text{step}} \cdot \frac{1}{2} l_0 \left( \beta_{\text{gspt}} + \sqrt{D_{\text{reg}}} \right)$$

* **Physical Effect:** When $\Delta$ is strongly negative (high stability), $H_{\text{step}} \to 0$ and $q^* \to 0$, suppressing turbulent production. When shear dominates ($\Delta > 0$), $H_{\text{step}} \to 1$, activating full turbulent production.
* **Numerical Effect:** The parameter $\xi$ smooths theHeaviside-like jump ($H_{\text{step}}$) into a $C^\infty$ manifold manifold transition, preventing Jacobian singular points.

## 2. Activation Gate & Diffusivity Formulation

### Lipschitz Activation Gate ($\Psi_{\text{gate}}$)

To prevent numerical chatter and non-physical background activity when $q^* \approx 0$, the turbulent energy scalar $\tilde{e}^* = (q^*)^2$ passes through a Lipschitz-continuous activation gate:

$$\Psi_{\text{gate}} = \frac{\sqrt{\tilde{e}^*}}{\sqrt{\tilde{e}^*} + \alpha_{\text{gate}}}$$

Where $\alpha_{\text{gate}}$ is the activation scale ($1.0 \times 10^{-3}$).

### Momentum Diffusivity ($K_m$)

The face eddy viscosity combines the regularized turbulent velocity scale with background mixing ($\delta$) and an interior mixing floor ($K_{\min,\text{interior}}$):

$$K_m = K_{\min,\text{interior}} + \ell_z \sqrt{\Psi_{\text{gate}} \tilde{e}^* + \delta}$$

### Heat Diffusivity ($K_h$) & Dynamic Prandtl Number ($\Pr_t$)

The turbulent Prandtl number varies dynamically with atmospheric stability $G$:

$$\Pr_t = \Pr_{t,\text{base}} + \Pr_{t,\text{slope}} \tanh\left(\max(0, G)\right)$$

$$K_h = \frac{K_m}{\max(\Pr_t, \varepsilon)}$$

## 3. Spatial Scaling & Dynamic Inversion Height

### Blackadar Mixing Length with Inversion Cap

The neutral mixing length $\ell_{\text{neutral}}$ follows Blackadar's asymptotic formulation up to the master length scale $l_0$, capped by an exponential decay scale ($h_{\text{eff}}$) and an interior floor ($\ell_{\min,\text{interior}}$):

$$\ell_{\text{neutral}} = \frac{\kappa z}{1 + \frac{\kappa z}{l_0}}$$

$$\ell_{z,\text{raw}} = \ell_{\text{neutral}} \exp\left(-\frac{z}{h_{\text{eff}}}\right)$$

$$\ell_z = \sqrt{\ell_{z,\text{raw}}^2 + \ell_{\min,\text{interior}}^2}$$

### Dynamic Non-Local Scale ($h_{\text{eff}}$)

When non-local scaling is enabled (`use_nonlocal_h = 1`), $h_{\text{eff}}$ adapts dynamically based on the local wind velocity scale $\vert{}\vec{V}\vert{}$ and the Coriolis parameter $f$:

$$h_{\text{nonlocal}} = \text{clamp}\left( \frac{\max\left(\vert{}\vec{V}\vert{}, u_{\text{floor}}\right)}{\max\left(\vert{}f\vert{}, f_{\text{floor}}\right)}, h_{\min}, h_{\max} \right)$$

$$h_{\text{eff}} = (1 - w_h) h_{\text{local}} + w_h h_{\text{nonlocal}}$$

## 4. Stability Saturation & Surface Exchange Safeguards

### Bounded Stability Operator ($G$)

To prevent runaway growth under strong surface inversions ($\partial \theta / \partial z \gg 0$), the raw stability argument is saturated via a $C^\infty$ hyperbolic tangent:

$$\text{arg} = \text{clamp}\left( \beta_{\text{stab}} \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial z} \frac{\ell_z}{\theta_a}, -40.0, 40.0 \right)$$

$$G(x) = G_{\max} \tanh\left(\frac{\text{arg}}{G_{\max}}\right)$$

By bounding $G \le G_{\max}$ (default $1.0$), buoyancy destruction cannot grow infinitely, maintaining physical turbulence coupling during extreme inversions.

### Topological Surface Exchange Floor ($K_{h,\text{surf}}$)

At the land-atmosphere interface, the surface thermal exchange coefficient uses a topological norm floor:

$$K_{h,\text{surf,raw}} = \frac{K_{m,\text{surf}}}{\Pr_{t,\text{surf}}}$$

$$K_{h,\text{surf}} = \sqrt{K_{h,\text{surf,raw}}^2 + K_{\text{exchange,min}}^2}$$

This guarantees that skin temperature $T_s$ and atmospheric air temperature $\theta_1$ maintain a non-zero thermal link ($K_{\text{exchange,min}} \approx 1.0 \times 10^{-4} \text{ m}^2/\text{s}$), eliminating numerical decoupling and spurious runaway cooling spirals.

## 🔬 Core Mathematical Breakdown

### 1. Proof Mechanics of Forward Invariance (Theorem 1)

The proof addresses a fundamental flaw in traditional SCM formulations: the non-physical collapse of TKE into negative values ($e < -\delta$) during strong stratification.

By transforming into shifted coordinates $\varphi = e + \delta \ge 0$, the fast ODE becomes:

$$\varepsilon \frac{d\varphi}{dt} = l_0 \Delta(\mathbf{y})\,\varphi\,\Psi(\varphi;\alpha) + \beta\varphi - \frac{\varphi^{3/2}}{l_0}$$

Evaluating this vector field at the boundary $\varphi = 0$:

* **Activation Gate:** $\Psi(0;\alpha) = 0 \implies l_0 \Delta \cdot 0 \cdot 0 = 0$
* **Amplification Term:** $\beta \cdot 0 = 0$
* **Dissipation Term:** $\frac{0^{3/2}}{l_0} = 0$

Thus, $\left.\frac{d\varphi}{dt}\right\vert{}_{\varphi=0} = 0$ identically. Because the vector field is locally Lipschitz ($C^1$), Picard-Lindelöf uniqueness guarantees that no forward trajectory initialized in the interior ($\varphi > 0$) can cross the boundary $\varphi = 0$.

```

        ^
        |   Forward Trajectories (φ(t) > 0)
        |      \         /
        |       \       /
        |        '----->
  ------+------------------------------------------> Time (t)
        |  INVARIANT BOUNDARY: dφ/dt |_(φ=0) = 0
  φ = 0 |========================================== (e = -δ Floor)
        |   Forbidden Sub-Floor Region (φ < 0)

```

### 2. $C^\infty$ Hyperbolic Embedding ($e^*_\xi$)

For diagnostic closures that require non-negative states without ruining solver Jacobians, the hyperbolic embedding:

$$e^*_{\xi}(\mathbf{y}) = \frac{1}{2}\left(e^*(\mathbf{y}) + \sqrt{(e^*(\mathbf{y}))^2 + \xi^2}\right)$$

replaces piecewise max-clipping ($\max(0, e^*)$):

* **Asymptotic Limit:** As $\xi \to 0$, $e^*_\xi \to \max(0, e^*)$.
* **Derivative Regularization:** The derivative $\frac{d e^*_\xi}{d e^*} = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 + \frac{e^*}{\sqrt{(e^*)^2 + \xi^2}}\right)$ remains strictly bounded between $0$ and $1$ for all $e^* \in \mathbb{R}$, eliminating non-differentiable $C^0$ kink points.

## Executive Summary

This project develops, validates, and automates a novel mathematical and numerical framework for modeling the atmospheric nocturnal **Stable Boundary Layer (SBL)** using **Geometric Singular Perturbation Theory (GSPT)**.

Traditional Single-Column Models (SCMs) rely on diagnostic Monin-Obukhov Similarity Theory (MOST) stability curves ($f_m(Ri), f_h(Ri)$) that introduce non-differentiable kinks, artificial truncation gates (e.g., hard `max(0, e)` logic), and numerical stiffness during turbulent collapse. This project bypasses empirical diagnostic curves entirely by formulating the SBL as a **4D fast-slow dynamical system** governed by a prognostic fast Turbulent Kinetic Energy (TKE) coordinate $e$.

By regularizing the fast-slow vector field with a $C^\infty$ smooth activation gate, the model guarantees forward domain invariance, preserves normal hyperbolicity off bifurcation loci, and maintains continuous dual-number propagation for forward-mode Automatic Differentiation (AD) and implicit $L$-stable ODE integration.

## 1. Theoretical & Mathematical Framework

### 1.1 Continuous & Reduced Governing Systems

The continuous 1D Single-Column Model maps horizontal momentum ($U, V$), potential temperature ($\theta$), and subgrid TKE ($e$) across vertical coordinate $z \in [0, H]$ with $H = 500\text{ m}$:

$$\frac{\partial U}{\partial t} = f(V - V_g) + \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \left( K_m \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right)$$

$$\frac{\partial V}{\partial t} = -f(U - U_g) + \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \left( K_m \frac{\partial V}{\partial z} \right)$$

$$\frac{\partial \theta}{\partial t} = \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \left( K_h \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial z} \right)$$

In the reduced 0D fast-slow formulation, the state vector $\mathbf{x} = (e, U, V, T_s)^T$ evolves on the non-negative state space $\mathcal{X} = \{ e \ge -\delta, T_s > 0 \}$:

$$\varepsilon \frac{de}{dt} = l_0 \Delta(U,V,T_s) \left( \frac{e+\delta}{\sqrt{e+\delta}+\alpha} \right) + \beta(e+\delta) - \frac{(e+\delta)^{3/2}}{l_0}$$

$$\frac{dU}{dt} = f(V - V_g) - \gamma \sqrt{e+\delta} \, U$$

$$\frac{dV}{dt} = -f(U - U_g) - \gamma \sqrt{e+\delta} \, V$$

$$\frac{dT_s}{dt} = \frac{1}{C_{\text{skin}}} \left[ R_{\downarrow} - \sigma_{\text{SB}} T_s^4 - \rho c_p C_H \sqrt{e+\delta} (T_s - T_a) - \frac{\lambda_s}{d_{\text{soil}}} (T_s - T_{\text{deep}}) \right]$$

where $\tilde{e} \equiv e + \delta \ge 0$ is the shifted TKE coordinate, $\delta > 0$ is the physical background floor, and $\gamma \equiv \frac{C_D \sqrt{U^2+V^2}}{h_{\mathrm{eff}}}$ bridges the bulk surface drag to the emergent layer height $h_{\mathrm{eff}}$.

The driving net forcing diagnostic balances mechanical shear production against thermal stratification:

$$\Delta(U,V,T_s) = \eta \, \gamma \, (U^2 + V^2) - K \, G(T_s)$$

where the surface stability function takes the analytical form:

$$G(T_s) = \exp \left( \frac{\beta(T_a - T_s)}{T_a} \right) - 1$$

---

### 1.2 Critical Manifold Geometry ($\mathcal{M}_0$) & Bifurcation Loci

In the singular limit $\varepsilon \to 0$, the fast equation relaxes instantaneously, defining the critical manifold $\mathcal{M}_0 = \{ (U,V,T_s,e) \mid F(q, \mathbf{y}) = 0 \}$ where $q = \sqrt{e+\delta}$. Outside the $\mathcal{O}(\alpha)$ activation zone, $F(q, \mathbf{y}) = q \left[ l_0 \Delta(\mathbf{y}) + \beta q - \frac{q^2}{l_0} \right] = 0$, yielding three distinct invariant sheets:

1. **Laminar Sheet ($q_{\mathrm{lam}} = 0$):** Quiescent residual state ($e^* = -\delta$).
2. **Unstable Separatrix Sheet ($q_-$):** Basin threshold boundary:

$$q_-(\Delta) = \frac{\beta l_0 - l_0 \sqrt{\beta^2 + 4\Delta}}{2}$$


3. **Active Attracting Sheet ($q_+$):** Stable turbulent branch:

$$q_+(\Delta) = \frac{\beta l_0 + l_0 \sqrt{\beta^2 + 4\Delta}}{2}$$



```
       q (Turbulent Scale)
         ^
         |          /  q₊ (Active Attracting Sheet: M₀ᵃᶜᵗ)
         |         /
         |        *  Fold Point: (q_fold, Δ_fold)
         |       /
         |      /    q₋ (Unstable Separatrix Sheet)
  -------+-----*----------------------------------> Δ (Net Forcing)
         |    /  Δ = 0 (Transcritical Ignition Point)
         |   /
         |  o________ q_lam = 0 (Laminar Residual Sheet: M₀ˡᵃᵐ)
```

#### Analytical Bifurcation Invariants

Solving the fold conditions $\mathcal{F}(q, \mathbf{y}) = 0$ and $\partial_q \mathcal{F}(q, \mathbf{y}) = 0$ yields closed-form invariants for saddle-node collapse:

$$q_{\mathrm{fold}} = \frac{\beta l_0}{2}, \qquad e_{\mathrm{fold}} = \frac{\beta^2 l_0^2}{4} - \delta, \qquad \Delta_{\mathrm{fold}} = -\frac{\beta^2}{4}$$

* **Fold Catastrophe ($\mathcal{C}_{\text{fold}}$):** When thermal cooling reduces net forcing below $\Delta_{\text{fold}} = -\frac{\beta^2}{4}$, normal hyperbolicity is lost, triggering rapid collapse to the laminar sheet.
* **Transcritical Re-ignition ($\Delta = 0$):** When shear production overcomes stratification ($\Delta > 0$), $q_-$ crosses zero, turning $q_{\mathrm{lam}}$ repelling and allowing turbulence to grow deterministically without ad-hoc restart switches.

### 1.3 Core Mathematical Theorems & Proofs

> **Theorem 1 (Forward Invariance of the Physical Domain).**
> Consider the regularized fast sub-dynamics $\varepsilon \frac{de}{dt} = l_0 \Delta(\mathbf{y}) (e+\delta) \Psi(e+\delta; \alpha) + \beta (e+\delta) - \frac{(e+\delta)^{3/2}}{l_0}$ with $\Psi(0; \alpha) = 0$ and $\Psi \in C^1$. If $e(0) \ge -\delta$, the subgrid phase space domain $\Omega = \{ e \in \mathbb{R} \mid e \ge -\delta \}$ is strictly positively invariant under forward flow.
> *Proof.* Define shifted coordinate $\varphi = e + \delta \ge 0$. The ODE becomes $\varepsilon \frac{d\varphi}{dt} = l_0 \Delta \varphi \Psi(\varphi; \alpha) + \beta \varphi - \frac{\varphi^{3/2}}{l_0}$. Evaluating the vector field at boundary $\varphi = 0$ yields $\left. \frac{d\varphi}{dt} \right\vert{}_{\varphi=0} = 0$. Because $\frac{d}{d\varphi}(\varphi^{3/2}) = \frac{3}{2}\sqrt{\varphi} \to 0$ as $\varphi \to 0^+$, the right-hand side is $C^1$ regular at $\varphi = 0$. By Picard–Lindelöf uniqueness, no forward trajectory initialized in $\varphi(0) > 0$ can cross $\varphi = 0$, proving $e(t) \ge -\delta$ for all $t > 0$. $\blacksquare$

> **Proposition 1 (Thermal Downward Translation).**
> Let $\partial G / \partial T_s < 0$ everywhere in the physical domain. Continuous radiative skin cooling ($T_s \downarrow$) induces a uniform, monotonic downward geometric translation of the active critical manifold sheet along the fast coordinate axis.
> *Proof.* On $M_0^{\text{act}}$, $e^* = q_+^2 - \delta$. Differentiating with respect to $T_s$:
>
> $$\frac{\partial e^*}{\partial T_s} = 2 q_+ \frac{\partial q_+}{\partial \Delta} \frac{\partial \Delta}{\partial T_s}$$
>
>
>
> Given $G(T_s) = \exp\left(\frac{\beta(T_a - T_s)}{T_a}\right) - 1$, we have $\frac{\partial G}{\partial T_s} = -\frac{\beta}{T_a} \exp\left(\frac{\beta(T_a - T_s)}{T_a}\right) < 0$. Thus $\frac{\partial \Delta}{\partial T_s} = -K \frac{\partial G}{\partial T_s} > 0$. Since $\frac{\partial q_+}{\partial \Delta} = \frac{l_0}{\sqrt{\beta^2+4\Delta}} > 0$, it follows that $\frac{\partial e^*}{\partial T_s} > 0$. Monotonic cooling ($\Delta T_s < 0$) forces $\Delta e^* < 0$, translating $M_0^{\text{act}}$ downward toward the fold boundary. $\blacksquare$

## 2. Numerical Architecture & Julia Solver Pipeline

### 2.1 Mitigation of Off-Diagonal Coupling Singularity

In un-regularized models, slow momentum drag scales with $\sqrt{e+\delta}$. Differentiating zonal momentum tendency with respect to shifted coordinate $\varphi = e+\delta$ exposes an off-diagonal Jacobian coupling singularity near collapse:

$$J_{\text{coupling}} = \frac{\partial}{\partial \varphi} \left( \frac{dU}{dt} \right) = -C_D \frac{\sqrt{U^2+V^2}}{h_{\mathrm{eff}}} \frac{U}{2\sqrt{\varphi}} \implies \lim_{\varphi \to 0^+} \left\vert{} J_{\text{coupling}} \right\vert{} = +\infty$$

To eliminate this singularity, the implementation embeds the smooth safeguard gate $\Psi(\varphi; \alpha) = \frac{\sqrt{\varphi}}{\sqrt{\varphi} + \alpha}$:

1. **Asymptotic Recovery:** For active states ($\varphi \gg \alpha$), $\Psi \to 1$, preserving exact GSPT dynamics.
2. **Singularity Interception:** As $\varphi \to 0$, $\Psi \to 0$ faster than $\frac{1}{\sqrt{\varphi}}$ diverges, clamping the Jacobian entries.
3. **AD Differentiability:** Replaces non-differentiable $C^0$ conditional branching with $C^\infty$ smooth operators, ensuring dual numbers (`ForwardDiff.Dual`) propagate without `NaN` singularities.

---

### 2.2 Vectorized Banded Matrix Strategy

For the 1D spatial SCM discretization ($N = 250$ vertical nodes, $\Delta z = 2.0\text{ m}$), state variables are block-interleaved to maximize cache locality and structure Jacobian sparsity:

$$\mathbf{X} = [T_s, U_1, V_1, \theta_1, U_2, V_2, \theta_2, \dots, U_N, V_N, \theta_N]^T$$

A 3-point central difference diffusion operator maps to a tight **BandedMatrix** prototype ($W = 7$ sub/super-diagonals). Pairing this band structure with matrix coloring algorithms drops Newton evaluation cost during implicit integration from $\mathcal{O}(N^3)$ dense matrix operations to an $\mathcal{O}(1)$ evaluation pass.

```julia
using DifferentialEquations
using LinearAlgebra
using ForwardDiff

# ====================================================================
# Physical & Numerical Parameters (Active Dataset Baseline)
# ====================================================================
const Ug        = 6.0          # Zonal geostrophic wind (m/s)
const Vg        = 0.0          # Meridional geostrophic wind (m/s)
const Ta        = 265.0        # Reference air temperature (K)
const Tdeep     = 270.0        # Deep soil/ice core temperature (K)
const Rdown     = 180.0        # Downward longwave radiation (W/m^2)
const f_cor     = 1.0e-4       # Coriolis parameter (s^-1)

const epsilon   = 0.01         # Fast-slow timescale ratio (eps << 1)
const delta     = 1.0e-4       # Background TKE floor (m^2/s^2)
const K_buoy    = 0.035        # Buoyant destruction scale (m/s^2)
const beta      = 2.1          # Stability parameter
const h_bl      = 100.0        # Reference scale height (m)
const l0        = 15.0         # Master mixing length scale (m)
const CH        = 1.2e-3       # Thermal exchange coefficient
const eta       = 1.5          # Shear efficiency (SHEBA tuned)

const sigma_SB  = 5.670374e-8  # Stefan-Boltzmann constant (W/m^2/K^4)
const lambda_s  = 0.2          # Thermal conductivity (W/m/K)
const d_soil    = 0.1          # Substrate coupling depth (m)
const rho_cp    = 1200.0       # Volumetric heat capacity of air (J/m^3/K)
const C_skin    = 2.0e4        # Skin thermal capacity (J/m^2/K)

const alpha_safe = 1.0e-6      # Safeguard gate scale
const ts_min     = 220.0       # Physical floor limit (K)

# ====================================================================
# Vector Field Definition (AD-Safe, Type-Generic Structure)
# ====================================================================
function gspt_sbl_dynamics!(dx, x, p, t)
    # State mapping: x = [e, U, V, Ts]
    e  = x[1]
    U  = x[2]
    V  = x[3]
    Ts = max(x[4], ts_min)

    # Shifted coordinate
    phi = e + delta

    # Emergent scale height with explicit runtime clamp logic
    wind_speed = sqrt(U^2 + V^2 + 1.0e-6)
    h_eff = clamp(h_bl * (wind_speed / Ug), 20.0, 400.0)
    gamma_eff = 1.4e-3 * (100.0 / h_eff)

    # Smooth safeguard gate
    psi = sqrt(phi + 1.0e-15) / (sqrt(phi + 1.0e-15) + alpha_safe)

    # Auxiliary stability terms
    G_Ts = exp(beta * (Ta - Ts) / Ta) - 1.0
    shear_prod = eta * gamma_eff * (U^2 + V^2)
    buoy_dest  = K_buoy * G_Ts
    Delta      = shear_prod - buoy_dest

    # AD-safe regularized square root (+1.0e-15 prevents NaN derivatives)
    sqrt_phi_reg = sqrt(phi + 1.0e-15)

    # Surface Energy Balance
    Rn = Rdown - sigma_SB * (Ts^4)
    H  = rho_cp * CH * sqrt_phi_reg * (Ts - Ta)
    G  = lambda_s * (Ts - Tdeep) / d_soil

    # Evaluated Tendencies
    dx[1] = (1.0 / epsilon) * (l0 * Delta * phi * psi + beta * phi - (phi^(1.5)) / l0)
    dx[2] = f_cor * (V - Vg) - gamma_eff * sqrt_phi_reg * U
    dx[3] = -f_cor * (U - Ug) - gamma_eff * sqrt_phi_reg * V
    dx[4] = (1.0 / C_skin) * (Rn - H - G)

    return nothing
end

# Execution: Rodas5P (5th-order A-L-stable Rosenbrock method with AD)
x0 = [0.05, 5.0, -1.0, 250.0]
tspan = (0.0, 43200.0)
prob = ODEProblem(gspt_sbl_dynamics!, x0, tspan)
sol = solve(prob, Rodas5P(autodiff=true), reltol=1.0e-6, abstol=1.0e-8)

```

The issue with the Gradient Richardson number ($Ri_g = N^2 / S^2$) blowing up is one of the most persistent numerical headaches in atmospheric modeling. When wind shear drops ($S^2 \to 0$) under strong radiative cooling ($N^2 > 0$), $Ri_g \to \infty$.

Operational weather and climate models (e.g., WRF, ECMWF, MPAS) historically patch this with hard conditional truncations like `Ri = min(Ri, Ri_max)`. These IF-statements introduce non-differentiable kinks that stall implicit ODE solvers, cause runaway ground-surface cooling, and force artificial turbulence collapse.

To upgrade stability or shear functions in existing models **without rewriting their core mixing schemes**, you can use three drop-in mathematical strategies.

---

## 1. The Bounded Stability Coordinate ($\chi$)

Instead of passing an unbounded domain $Ri_g \in [0, \infty)$ into stability functions, map the physical state onto a compact, bounded interval $\chi \in [0, 1]$.

Define the normalized shear capacity $\chi$:

$$\chi = \frac{S^2}{S^2 + \lambda N^2} = \frac{1}{1 + \lambda Ri_g}$$

where $\lambda \approx 1.0 - 4.0$ is a tuning parameter matching neutral/stable transition scales.

* **Neutral Limit ($N^2 \to 0$):** $\chi \to 1$
* **Unbounded Stable Limit ($Ri_g \to \infty$):** $\chi \to 0$

### Drop-in Implementation

In legacy model code, replace $f(Ri_g)$ with a smooth function $g(\chi)$:

$$K_m = l^2 S \cdot g(\chi), \quad \text{where } g(\chi) = \chi^p + \frac{K_{\text{laminar}}}{l^2 S}$$

Because $\chi$ is strictly bounded in $[0, 1]$, zero-shear division is mathematically impossible, and diffusivities decay smoothly to residual background floors.

## 2. Hyperbolic Regularization ($Ri_{\text{eff}}$)

If an existing model codebase strictly requires passing a variable named `Ri` into legacy lookup tables or closures, replace the raw gradient ratio with a $C^\infty$ smooth hyperbolic saturation function.

Replace `Ri = N2 / max(S2, 1e-10)` and `Ri = min(Ri, Ri_c)` with:

$$Ri_{\text{eff}} = Ri_c \tanh\left( \frac{N^2}{Ri_c (S^2 + \delta_s)} \right)$$

where $Ri_c \approx 0.25$ (the classical critical Richardson number) and $\delta_s \approx 10^{-8} \text{ s}^{-2}$ is a numerical regularization floor.

### Properties

* **Small $Ri_g$:** $Ri_{\text{eff}} \approx Ri_g$ (retains standard Monin-Obukhov behavior near neutral).
* **Large $Ri_g$:** As $S^2 \to 0$, $Ri_{\text{eff}} \to Ri_c$ asymptotically without sharp conditional branches.
* **Solver Friendly:** Differentiable everywhere ($\frac{d Ri_{\text{eff}}}{dS}$ is smooth), preventing Newton-Raphson Jacobian failures in implicit land-surface couplings.

## 3. Net Production Difference ($\Delta$) — The GSPT Approach

The root cause of $Ri_g$ blow-up is using a **ratio** ($N^2 / S^2$). In GSPT, stability is evaluated as an algebraic **difference** between shear production and buoyancy consumption:

$$\Delta = S^2 - \frac{N^2}{Pr_t}$$

where $Pr_t$ is the turbulent Prandtl number.

To calculate diffusivities $K_m$ directly:

$$K_m = l_0^2 \sqrt{\max(\Delta, 0) + \delta}$$

### Advantages for Existing Models

* Completely removes $S^2$ from the denominator.
* Replaces complex stability lookup tables with a single vectorizable linear combination: `Delta = S2 - N2 / Pr_t`.
* Unifies the laminar transition threshold cleanly at $\Delta = 0$.

⸻

1. Bounded coordinate \chi
This is probably the most mathematically elegant of the three.
Defining

* compact domain
* bounded derivatives
* no division-by-zero singularity
* monotonic mapping

One thing I would avoid saying is

Strictly speaking, the computation still involves S^2+\lambda N^2. The denominator can only vanish if both shear and stratification vanish simultaneously. In practice this is rarely problematic, but for complete robustness one would implement
\chi=\frac{S^2+\delta}{S^2+\lambda N^2+\delta},
with a tiny positive \delta.
That gives a globally defined C^\infty map.

This is probably the easiest path for existing operational codes.

The classical critical Richardson number is not a hard physical upper bound.
Observations frequently report
Ri_g>1,\qquadRi_g>10,\qquadRi_g>100

Ri_c=0.25
should probably be described as
a numerical regularization parameter,

This is where the physics changes.

K_h=\frac{K_m}{Pr_t},

I think this is the strongest physical motivation.

replacing a ratio-based stability parameterization with a production-minus-dissipation closure.
That distinction matters.

### Level 2: Bounded Coordinate ($\chi$) — *Coordinate Compactification*

To prevent the denominator from vanishing in the rare double-zero limit where both shear and stratification collapse ($S^2 \to 0$ and $N^2 \to 0$), introduce a tiny $C^\infty$ positive offset $\delta > 0$:

$$\chi = \frac{S^2 + \delta}{S^2 + \lambda N^2 + \delta} \in (0, 1]$$

* **Domain:** Compactly maps the unbounded half-line $Ri_g \in [0, \infty)$ onto $(0, 1]$.
* **Global Smoothness:** Strictly $C^\infty$ across all physical states, completely removing division-by-zero singularities without conditional branching.

### Level 3: Production Difference ($\Delta$) — *Intrinsic Energetic Closure*

Rather than parameterizing stability as a dimensionless ratio ($N^2 / S^2$), express net turbulence production directly from the TKE budget balance:

$$\Delta = S^2 - \frac{N^2}{Pr_t}$$

$$K_m = l_0^2 \sqrt{\Delta_* + \delta}$$

* **Energetic Basis:** Directly represents the net forcing $P_s + B \propto K_m \Delta$.
* **Manifold Alignment:** Fully differentiable across the laminar-turbulent transition ($\Delta = 0$), matching the GSPT reduced-branch embedding architecture.
