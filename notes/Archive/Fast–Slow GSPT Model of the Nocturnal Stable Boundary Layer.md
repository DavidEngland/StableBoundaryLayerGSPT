**Fast–Slow GSPT Model of the Nocturnal Stable Boundary Layer**  
  
**State Variables**  
  
**Let**  
  
* e(t)** = turbulent kinetic energy (fast variable)**  
* S(t)** = vertical wind shear across the stable boundary layer**  
* T_s(t)** = surface skin temperature**  
* \Gamma(t)** = near-surface static stability (or inversion strength)**  
  
**The system evolves on**  
  
\mathcal X  
=  
\{(e,S,T_s,\Gamma)\}  
\subset  
\mathbb R^4.  
  
⸻  
  
**Time-scale Separation**  
  
**Introduce**  
  
0<\varepsilon\ll1,  
  
**representing the ratio**  
  
\varepsilon  
=  
\frac{\text{turbulence adjustment time}}  
{\text{surface cooling time}}.  
  
**Typically,**  
  
* **turbulence adjusts in minutes,**  
* **soil and radiative cooling evolve over tens of minutes to hours.**  
  
**Thus**  
  
* e** is fast,**  
* S,T_s,\Gamma** are slow.**  
  
⸻  
  
**Fast Equation (TKE)**  
  
**The regularized TKE equation is**  
  
\boxed{  
\varepsilon  
\frac{de}{dt}  
=  
\sqrt{e+\delta}  
\left(  
\sigma S^2  
-  
K\Gamma  
-  
\alpha e  
\right)  
}  
  
**where**  
  
* \sigma S^2** = mechanical production,**  
* K\Gamma** = buoyancy destruction,**  
* \alpha e** = dissipation,**  
* \delta>0** = background mixing.**  
  
**Define**  
  
\Delta  
=  
\sigma S^2-K\Gamma.  
  
**Then**  
  
\varepsilon\dot e  
=  
\sqrt{e+\delta}  
(\Delta-\alpha e).  
  
⸻  
  
**Slow Shear Equation**  
  
**The wind shear evolves because of geostrophic forcing while turbulence removes momentum.**  
  
**A minimal equation is**  
  
\boxed{  
\frac{dS}{dt}  
=  
F_g  
-  
\gamma  
\sqrt{e+\delta}\,S  
}  
  
**where**  
  
F_g  
  
**is the effective pressure-gradient forcing.**  
  
**The drag term represents turbulent momentum transport.**  
  
⸻  
  
**Surface Energy Budget**  
  
**McNider’s central contribution was recognizing that the land surface must evolve dynamically.**  
  
**The skin temperature satisfies**  
  
\boxed{  
C_s  
\frac{dT_s}{dt}  
=  
R_n(T_s)  
-  
H(e,S,\Gamma)  
-  
G(T_s)  
}  
  
**where**  
  
* R_n** = net radiation,**  
* H** = sensible heat flux,**  
* G** = ground heat flux.**  
  
**One convenient closure is**  
  
H  
=  
\rho c_p  
K_h  
\Gamma  
  
**with**  
  
K_h  
=  
c_h l_0  
\sqrt{e+\delta}.  
  
**Hence**  
  
H  
=  
\rho c_p  
c_h l_0  
\sqrt{e+\delta}  
\Gamma.  
  
⸻  
  
**Stability Evolution**  
  
**The inversion is generated by surface cooling and destroyed by turbulent mixing.**  
  
**A simple evolution equation is**  
  
\boxed{  
\frac{d\Gamma}{dt}  
=  
a(T_a-T_s)  
-  
b  
\sqrt{e+\delta}\,  
\Gamma  
}  
  
**where**  
  
 a  
  
**converts surface cooling into inversion strength,**  
  
**while**  
  
 b  
  
**represents turbulent erosion.**  
  
⸻
