When evaluating this unified function in standard double-precision arithmetic deep in the stable regime ($\text{Ri}_g > \text{Ri}_c$), the term $\left(1 - \frac{\text{Ri}_g}{\text{Ri}_c}\right)$ becomes a large negative number. Adding this value to the nearly identical positive radical forces the processor to subtract two large, similar numbers, leading to **catastrophic floating-point cancellation** that introduces arithmetic noise into the eddy diffusivity values.

To eliminate this vulnerability, we multiply the primary expression by its algebraic conjugate. This rationalizes the numerator and yields a robust, conditional implementation split cleanly across the physical threshold:

$$\text{For } \text{Ri}_g \le \text{Ri}_c: \quad S_m(\text{Ri}_g) = \frac{1}{4} \left[ \left(1 - \frac{\text{Ri}_g}{\text{Ri}_c}\right) + \sqrt{\left(1 - \frac{\text{Ri}_g}{\text{Ri}_c}\right)^2 + \epsilon} \;\right]^2$$

$$\text{For } \text{Ri}_g > \text{Ri}_c: \quad S_m(\text{Ri}_g) = \frac{1}{4} \left[ \frac{\epsilon}{\sqrt{\left(1 - \frac{\text{Ri}_g}{\text{Ri}_c}\right)^2 + \epsilon} + \left(\frac{\text{Ri}_g}{\text{Ri}_c} - 1\right)} \;\right]^2$$

Because the terms in the denominator of the supercritical branch are strictly additive, subtraction is completely eliminated. The function remains perfectly well-conditioned and continuous across the boundary, evaluating to exactly $\frac{\epsilon}{4}$ at the precise interface where $\text{Ri}_g = \text{Ri}_c$.
