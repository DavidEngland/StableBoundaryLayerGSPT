### 1. Mathematical Validation of the Smooth Limiter

When implementing a smooth floor limiter for an equation like $\frac{de}{dt} = \frac{F}{\epsilon}$, you want to ensure the regularized derivative smoothly approaches zero as $e$ hits the floor $e_{\text{floor}} = -\delta + \epsilon_{\text{pos}}$.

A robust approach is to scale the negative component of the derivative using a hyperbolic tangent or algebraic smoothing gate:

$$\frac{de}{dt}_{\text{smooth}} = \text{de\_dt} \cdot \sigma_{\text{gate}}(e)$$

Where the gate function goes from $1 \to 0$ as $e$ approaches $e_{\text{floor}}$:

$$\sigma_{\text{gate}}(e) = \frac{1}{2} \left[ 1 + \tanh\left( \frac{e - e_{\text{floor}}}{\alpha} \right) \right]$$

Here, $\alpha$ represents your tunable `smooth_eps_width`. When $e \gg e_{\text{floor}}$, $\sigma_{\text{gate}} \approx 1$ and the system is untouched. As $e \to e_{\text{floor}}$, it scales down negative derivatives perfectly without discontinuities.
