## 1. TKE-Based Eddy Diffusivity (1.5-Order Closure)

In TKE-based models, $K_m$ is determined by combining a characteristic turbulent velocity scale (derived from the Turbulent Kinetic Energy, $e$) and a characteristic mixing length scale ($l$):

$$K_m = c_k \cdot l \cdot \sqrt{e} \cdot f_m(Ri_g)$$

* **$e$ (TKE):** The turbulent kinetic energy state, typically governed by its own prognostic equation:

$$\frac{\partial e}{\partial t} = \underbrace{K_m \left[ \left(\frac{\partial U}{\partial z}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial z}\right)^2 \right]}_{\text{Shear Production } (\mathcal{P}_m)} - \underbrace{K_h \frac{g}{\theta_0} \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial z}}_{\text{Buoyancy Production/Destruction } (\mathcal{B})} - \underbrace{\varepsilon}_{\text{Dissipation}} + \text{Diffusion}$$


* **$\sqrt{e}$:** Serves as the characteristic velocity scale of velocity fluctuations ($u'$).
* **$l$:** The turbulent master length scale (or mixing length).
* **$c_k$:** A dimensionless empirical parameter (often around $0.1$–$0.55$).
* **$f_m(Ri_g)$:** A stability function that damps diffusivity under stable stratification ($Ri_g > 0$).

---

## 2. Thermal Diffusivity ($K_h$) and the Turbulent Prandtl Number ($Pr_t$)

Thermal diffusivity $K_h$ is related to momentum diffusivity $K_m$ through the **turbulent Prandtl number** ($Pr_t$):

$$K_h = \frac{K_m}{Pr_t}$$

In neutral atmospheric conditions, $Pr_t \approx 0.7$–$0.85$. However, under stable boundary layer conditions, $Pr_t$ is often formulated dynamically as a function of local stability (e.g., the Gradient Richardson number $Ri_g$):

$$Pr_t(Ri_g) = Pr_{t,0} + \beta \cdot Ri_g$$

where $Ri_g$ is defined as:

$$Ri_g = \frac{\frac{g}{\theta_0} \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial z}}{\left( \frac{\partial U}{\partial z} \right)^2 + \left( \frac{\partial V}{\partial z} \right)^2}$$

---

## 3. Mixing Length Formulations ($l$)

The length scale $l$ determines how large turbulent eddies can grow before dissipating. Common formulations include:

1. **Blackadar Length Scale:**

$$l = \frac{\kappa z}{1 + \frac{\kappa z}{l_0}}$$



where $\kappa \approx 0.4$ is the von Kármán constant and $l_0$ is the asymptotic length scale in the free atmosphere.
2. **Stability-Corrected Length Scale (Mellor–Yamada / Deardorff):**
Under strong nocturnal cooling ($\frac{\partial \theta}{\partial z} > 0$), buoyancy limits vertical eddy size, capping the mixing length by the buoyancy scale $l_b$:

$$l_b = c_b \cdot \frac{\sqrt{e}}{N}, \quad \text{where } N = \sqrt{\frac{g}{\theta_0} \frac{\partial \theta}{\partial z}}$$



---

## 4. Alternative First-Order Diagnostic Closure (K-Theory)

If TKE ($e$) is not solved prognostically, $K_m$ can be diagnosed strictly from local wind shear $S = \left[ \left(\frac{\partial U}{\partial z}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial z}\right)^2 \right]^{1/2}$ using classic local closure (e.g., Louis 1979 or Blackadar 1979):

$$K_m = l^2 \cdot S \cdot F_m(Ri_g)$$

$$K_h = l^2 \cdot S \cdot F_h(Ri_g)$$
